Blondy's Baked Goods App

An E-Commerce app designed to serve allergy-friendly baked goods to the community.

Who is Blondy?

A single mom that found new life in baking. She started her own baking company and started selling at Market of Choice and local community coffee shops. Wants an app built off of a design to help extend her goods to customers that can't make it into grocery stores or coffee shops but still want an allergy friendly treat.

The problem to solve?

There is a rising need and want for baked goods that are allergy-friendly within the community of Oregon. Specifically, coffee shops either do not have any or lack good gluten and dairy-free options. Demand grew and her customers wanted an easier way to order from her.

The Design Process



1. Think

In the beginning stages of the project I did the visual design, research for app features, and wireframe sketches. The first step in the project was to create a consistent look and feel for the app. Working with a brand manager we were able to come up with typography, color palette, iconography, and a brand logo.



App Features



After the design was done the next step was to conduct research on what features users would want in the app. Going to local coffee shops I conducted interviews with various customers and gathered information on what features users like and dislike in apps similar to this one.

Different Themes


Changing the theme of the app to either dark or light.

Language Translator


Providing users the ability to change the text to their native language.

Audio Screen Reader


An audio playback of the text on the screen. Users can hit a button to listen to an audio version of the text.

Text Magnifier


A magnifier to enhance the size of the text to be able to read it easier.

Favorites List


Users can save their favorites that they like for quick ordering.

Monthly Vouchers


After each purchase a user can choose to sign up for emails and receive a free product from Blondyâ€™s if they purchase a coffee in one of the local shops.



Low-wireframe sketches


Now that the research and build features are done I can move onto sketching out by hand the wireframes. This step of the design process really helps me save time by mapping out before hand the layout of the app.



2. Make



Wireframes

With the sketches completed I was able to move onto my digital wireframes. I decided to use Figma as I was more familiar with it at the time and could work faster with it, saving time and money.



Mockups

With everything in place it was time for the high-fidelity mockups. Everyone was very pleased on how they turned out and it was a great feeling seeing the idea come to reality. All that was next was to create a functional prototype to test out on users.

The Prototype

Having the high-fidelity mockups completed and green lighted it was finally time to create a working prototype. Using Figma, I created 3 different prototypes: a dark theme, a light theme, and the default theme. Below is a final working version. Viewing it in full screen is preferred but not necessary



3. Check



User reactions & test feedback

A working prototype now in hand it was back to the local coffee shops I go. Conducting more interviews I was able to get crucial in depth feedback on the apps overall user experience.
